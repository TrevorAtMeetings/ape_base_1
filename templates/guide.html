02

Pump Selection Process

Master the core functionality with detailed guidance on making optimal pump selections.

input Input Form Guide

Required Fields
Flow Rate (m³/hr)
The volume of liquid the pump must move per hour
Example: 342 m³/hr for large building HVAC
Total Head (m)
Total pressure the pump must overcome
Example: 27.4 m for 3-story building
Optional Fields
Application Type
Helps AI understand specific requirements
Options: Axial Flow, End Suction, HSC, Multi-Stage, VTP
Project Details
Customer and project information for reports
Used in PDF documentation

analytics Understanding Your Results

stars
Suitability Score

90-100%: Excellent match, highly recommended
75-89%: Good match with minor compromises
60-74%: Acceptable but consider alternatives
Below 60%: Poor match, review requirements

speed
Efficiency Rating

Shows pump efficiency at your operating point. Higher efficiency means lower energy costs. Look for values above 75% for optimal performance.

warning
Operating Point Analysis

Green indicators show the pump operates within optimal range. Yellow indicates acceptable performance. Red warns of potential issues.

03

Charts & Analysis

Understand performance curves and make data-driven decisions with interactive visualizations.

show_chart

Head-Flow Curve

Shows how pump head varies with flow rate. Your operating point should fall within the efficient range of the curve.

trending_up

Efficiency Curve

Displays pump efficiency across different flow rates. Aim for the peak efficiency zone to minimize energy costs.

power

Power Curve

Shows power consumption at different operating points. Use this to estimate energy costs and motor sizing.

water_drop

NPSHr Curve

Net Positive Suction Head required. Ensure your system's NPSH available exceeds this value to prevent cavitation.

touch_app Interactive Features

Zoom & Pan

Use mouse wheel to zoom and click-drag to pan around charts for detailed examination.

Curve Selection

Click different impeller sizes to see how curves change and find the optimal size.

Operating Point

Red dot shows your exact operating point on all curves for easy reference.

Export Charts

Save charts as images or include them in PDF reports for presentations.
